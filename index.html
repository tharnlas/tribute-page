Mary Jackson

Mary Jackson

Mathematician and Aerospace Engineer

Timeline

1921 - Born on April 9 to Ella and Frank Winston.

- Graduated from George P. Phenix Training School, an all-black school.

1942 - Graduated from Hampton Institute with Bachelor's Degrees in Mathematics and Physical Science.

1942-1943 - Taught math at a black school in Calvert County, Maryland.

1951 - Recruited by the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics as a research mathematician at the Langley Research Center in Hampton, VA. Worked in the West Area Computing Section, which was segregated and consisted entirely of African-Americans.

1953 - Worked in the Supersonic Pressure Tunnel. Successfully petitioned the City of Hampton to take graduate courses in math and physics for all-white students. Became NASA's first black female engineer after completing the courses.

1985 - Retired from NASA.

2005 - Passed away on February 11 at age 83.
